# Terrain Tile — Mountain (Variant 2)

## Subject
A rugged rocky formation viewed from isometric perspective. Two adjacent stone pillars with a narrow gap between them — a natural pass or crevice. The rock is darker and more weathered than variant 1, with deeper erosion grooves. A small overhang or cave-like shadow at the base of one pillar. Loose boulders scattered around the formation. No vegetation, no human trace.

## Human Presence
None. Pure geology. No roads, no markers, no construction debris. These rocks have never been surveyed or mapped for development.

## Hex Shape
Flat-top hexagon tile, isometric perspective. The rock pillars rise above the hex boundary. The base with boulders fits within the hex footprint.

## Color Palette — Ash & Ember
- Rock faces: dark iron ore (#505058), slightly warmer tone than v1
- Deep crevice shadow: near-black
- Erosion grooves: medium grey lines
- Boulders at base: varied dark grey tones
- Overhang shadow: very dark, suggesting depth

## Art Style
Isometric hex tile sprite. Visually distinct from mountain_v1 — the twin-pillar formation creates a different silhouette. Still reads as "impassable mountain terrain" at game zoom. Angular, flat-shaded, geological. Heavy and imposing.

## World Context
Ash & Ember setting. Ancient rock formations that blocked the expansion routes. Surveyors marked these as "impassable — reroute" on their maps. Now the surveyors are gone but the rocks remain.

## Output
- Transparent PNG
- Isometric hex tile, approximately 120x140 pixels
- Flat-top hexagon orientation
- Rock pillars extend above hex top edge for visual height
- No units, no structures — terrain only
