# Terrain Tile — City (Variant 1)

## Subject
An urban ruin viewed from isometric perspective. A multi-story concrete building shell — walls standing but no roof, no windows, just rectangular openings. Exposed rebar poking out from broken concrete edges at the top floors. Hanging electrical cables droop from one side of the building to a broken utility pole. A rusted dumpster sits at ground level next to the building. Debris (concrete chunks, twisted metal) scattered on the ground. Faded graffiti tag on the lower wall.

## Human Presence
Heavy. This was an active construction zone — the building was being erected when everything stopped. The dumpster, cables, graffiti, and debris all speak to dense human activity that ceased abruptly. The building is a skeleton, frozen mid-construction.

## Hex Shape
Flat-top hexagon tile, isometric perspective. The building rises well above the hex boundary — tallest terrain type alongside mountains. The ground-level debris and dumpster fit within the hex footprint.

## Color Palette — Ash & Ember
- Concrete walls: ember orange-grey (#7A6048), warm but muted
- Exposed rebar: dark rust brown
- Hanging cables: black, thin lines
- Dumpster: dark green-grey, heavily rusted
- Ground debris: mixed grey and brown concrete chunks
- Graffiti: faded teal or copper, barely visible
- Window openings: deep shadow, near-black

## Art Style
Isometric hex tile sprite. The building should feel unfinished — not bombed, not old, just never completed. Clean concrete edges where construction stopped, ragged broken edges where materials degraded since. The vibe is "construction site abandoned overnight." Gritty, urban, dense with detail but readable at game zoom.

## World Context
Ash & Ember setting. This was a residential or commercial building under construction during the hyper-expansion boom. Workers left mid-shift. The crane was still attached (now rusted and frozen). Floors were poured but walls never finished. Years of weather have begun the slow demolition that the builders never completed.

## Output
- Transparent PNG
- Isometric hex tile, approximately 120x140 pixels
- Flat-top hexagon orientation
- Building extends above hex top edge for vertical presence
- No units — terrain only
