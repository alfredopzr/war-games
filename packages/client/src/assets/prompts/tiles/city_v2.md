# Terrain Tile — City (Variant 2)

## Subject
An urban ruin viewed from isometric perspective. A shorter, wider structure — a partially built commercial complex or strip mall. Two stories max. The roof is a flat concrete slab with exposed structural beams visible beneath. A collapsed section on one side shows the interior: floor tiles, a counter, rusted metal shelving. A faded business sign (text unreadable) hangs at an angle from the front. Weeds push through cracks in the concrete apron around the building.

## Human Presence
Heavy. This was closer to completion than city_v1 — interior finishing had begun before abandonment. The sign, shelving, and floor tiles show commercial intent. Now it's a shell being slowly reclaimed.

## Hex Shape
Flat-top hexagon tile, isometric perspective. Shorter than city_v1 — this is a low-rise structure. The building fits mostly within the hex height but edges may extend slightly.

## Color Palette — Ash & Ember
- Concrete and walls: ember orange-grey (#7A6048)
- Structural beams: dark steel grey
- Collapsed section: lighter interior colors, exposed pale concrete
- Sign: faded, muted color with rust stains
- Weeds: dark olive-green, sparse
- Floor tiles (visible in collapsed area): cracked cream or grey

## Art Style
Isometric hex tile sprite. Visually distinct from city_v1 — wider and lower instead of tall and narrow. Different stage of construction/decay. Reads as "urban terrain" at game zoom but offers visual variety when placed alongside city_v1. Gritty, abandoned, detailed but not cluttered.

## World Context
Ash & Ember setting. A strip mall or commercial complex that was nearly finished when the collapse hit. The sign was installed, shelving delivered, floors tiled — then everyone left. The roof leaks, one wall has caved in, and weeds are claiming the parking apron.

## Output
- Transparent PNG
- Isometric hex tile, approximately 120x140 pixels
- Flat-top hexagon orientation
- No units — terrain only
