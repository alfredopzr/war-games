# Terrain Tile — Mountain (Variant 1)

## Subject
A jagged rocky peak viewed from isometric perspective. Raw stone surfaces with sharp angular faces — fractured geological layers visible in cross-section. Wind erosion has carved horizontal striations into the rock faces. Small loose rocks and scree at the base of the peak. No vegetation, no human structures — pure geology. The peak rises significantly above the hex base plane.

## Human Presence
None. This is untouched geological terrain. The construction boom never reached here — too steep, too remote, too expensive to develop. Pure raw earth.

## Hex Shape
Flat-top hexagon tile, isometric perspective. The mountain peak rises well above the hex boundary — significant vertical height. The base fits within the hex footprint but the peak extends upward, giving the tile more vertical presence than other terrain types.

## Color Palette — Ash & Ember
- Rock faces: dark iron ore (#505058), with lighter and darker angular facets
- Erosion striations: slightly lighter grey lines across rock faces
- Shadows: deep charcoal-black in crevices
- Scree at base: mixed small stones in grey-brown tones
- No green, no vegetation colors

## Art Style
Isometric hex tile sprite. The mountain should feel heavy, imposing, and geological. Angular flat-shaded rock faces — like a low-poly 3D mountain rendered to 2D. Sharp edges, clear faceting. No soft gradients. The stone reads as hard, cold, and ancient. Wind-carved but not smooth.

## World Context
Ash & Ember setting. These mountains predate and outlast the human construction boom. They are obstacles the builders couldn't overcome — too expensive to tunnel through, too high to build over. They remain as they always were, indifferent to the collapse.

## Output
- Transparent PNG
- Isometric hex tile, approximately 120x140 pixels
- Flat-top hexagon orientation
- Mountain peak extends above hex top edge for visual height
- No units, no structures — terrain only
