# Terrain Tile — Plains (Variant 2)

## Subject
A flat, scorched plain viewed from isometric perspective. Compacted dirt with a fine layer of ash-grey dust. No road markings in this variant — just open, empty ground. A few scattered larger rocks (fist-sized) break the monotony. Very faint tire tracks pressed into the dry earth, partially filled by wind-blown dust. A single small metallic debris piece (bolt, washer, or nail) glints on the surface.

## Human Presence
Minimal. Tire tracks and a single piece of construction debris are the only signs. This land was surveyed but never developed.

## Hex Shape
Flat-top hexagon tile, isometric perspective. The tile sits flat on the ground plane with slight depth to the edges (thin visible side faces showing the hex is a slab, not paper-thin).

## Color Palette — Ash & Ember
- Base ground: ashen olive (#6A6A58)
- Dust layer: slightly lighter grey-olive
- Tire track depressions: slightly darker
- Rocks: dark grey-brown
- Metal debris: dull steel glint

## Art Style
Isometric hex tile sprite. Detailed enough to read at game zoom but not noisy. Visually distinct from plains_v1 but same terrain type — provides tiling variety. Flat, dry, empty feeling. Muted, desaturated palette.

## World Context
Ash & Ember setting. Open ground on the outskirts of the construction zone. Trucks drove through here during the boom. Now it's just packed earth and dust.

## Output
- Transparent PNG
- Isometric hex tile, approximately 120x140 pixels
- Flat-top hexagon orientation
- Suitable for seamless tiling alongside other hex terrain types
- No units, no structures — terrain only
