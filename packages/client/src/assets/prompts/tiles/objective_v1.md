# Terrain Tile — Objective (Command Tower)

## Subject
A command tower / communications hub viewed from isometric perspective. A squat, reinforced concrete bunker base with a tall antenna array rising from the roof — multiple antenna rods, a satellite dish, and blinking warning lights (red). This is the last working power source on the grid. A faint amber glow emanates from inside the bunker through narrow slit windows — power is on. Thick power cables run from the base into the ground. A small generator (visible exhaust vent, vibration lines) sits beside the bunker. The structure is functional, not ruined — maintained by whoever holds it.

## Human Presence
Active. Unlike all other terrain, this structure is operational. The lights work. The generator hums. Someone maintains this. It's the strategic prize both factions want.

## Hex Shape
Flat-top hexagon tile, isometric perspective. The antenna array extends high above the hex boundary — this is the tallest structure on the map. The bunker base and generator fit within the hex footprint.

## Color Palette — Ash & Ember
- Bunker walls: reinforced concrete grey with ember warmth
- Antenna array: dark steel, metallic
- Warning lights: molten gold (#C88A20), glowing red accents
- Interior glow through windows: warm amber/gold (#C88A20)
- Power cables: black, thick
- Generator: dark steel grey with exhaust heat shimmer
- Ground around base: packed, maintained concrete pad

## Art Style
Isometric hex tile sprite. This tile should stand out from all others — it's the only terrain that looks alive and powered. The amber glow and red warning lights create visual contrast against the muted Ash & Ember palette. The antenna gives it height dominance. It should feel important, strategic, worth fighting over.

## World Context
Ash & Ember setting. When the collapse hit, power grids failed globally. A few isolated nodes kept running on backup generators — this is one of them. The antenna can broadcast, the computers inside still function, and whoever controls this position controls communications and power for the surrounding region. Both Engineers and Caravaners need it.

## Output
- Transparent PNG
- Isometric hex tile, approximately 120x140 pixels
- Flat-top hexagon orientation
- Antenna extends well above hex top edge
- The amber glow effect should be part of the sprite (not added at render time, though an alpha pulse will be applied in PixiJS)
- No units — terrain only
