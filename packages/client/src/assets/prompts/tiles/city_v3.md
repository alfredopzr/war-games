# Terrain Tile — City (Variant 3)

## Subject
An urban ruin viewed from isometric perspective. An intersection of two half-built roads. Asphalt laid on one road, the other is just graded earth with curbs installed but no surface. A traffic light pole stands at the corner — installed but never powered, cables dangling loose. A concrete road barrier (jersey barrier) sits across the unpaved road. Manhole cover visible in the paved section. Scattered construction cones (faded orange, knocked over). The intersection is a snapshot of infrastructure frozen at different stages of completion.

## Human Presence
Heavy. Every element is human-made infrastructure. The traffic light, barriers, cones, curbs, manhole — all installed by workers who left and never came back. The asymmetry (one road paved, one not) tells the story of interrupted progress.

## Hex Shape
Flat-top hexagon tile, isometric perspective. This tile is flatter than city_v1/v2 — ground-level infrastructure rather than buildings. The traffic light pole provides modest vertical accent.

## Color Palette — Ash & Ember
- Asphalt road: dark grey with ember orange-grey (#7A6048) dust coating
- Graded earth road: packed brown-grey dirt
- Traffic light pole: dark steel grey, unlit signal housings
- Jersey barrier: pale concrete grey, stained
- Construction cones: faded orange, knocked on their sides
- Curbs: concrete, slightly lighter than road surface
- Manhole cover: dark iron with pattern

## Art Style
Isometric hex tile sprite. Provides ground-level urban variety — no tall buildings, just road infrastructure. The half-finished intersection is a microcosm of the Ash & Ember world. Reads as "city terrain" at game zoom. Different composition from city_v1 (tall building) and city_v2 (low-rise commercial).

## World Context
Ash & Ember setting. This intersection was being built to connect two new developments. One road got paved. The other was still being graded when work stopped. The traffic light was installed early (optimistic project management). Now it stands dark over an intersection that goes nowhere.

## Output
- Transparent PNG
- Isometric hex tile, approximately 120x140 pixels
- Flat-top hexagon orientation
- No units — terrain only
