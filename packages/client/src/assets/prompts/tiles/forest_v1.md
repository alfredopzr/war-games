# Terrain Tile — Forest (Variant 1)

## Subject
A dense cluster of charred, dark trees viewed from isometric perspective. The canopy is thick but damaged — some branches are bare and blackened, others hold dark green foliage. The trees are closely packed, creating a dark mass. At the base, a forgotten road sign is half-buried among exposed roots — tilted at an angle, text unreadable, rusted metal post. Ground cover is dark leaf litter and moss-covered fallen branches.

## Human Presence
Forgotten traces. The road sign is being swallowed by the forest. Roots have cracked and lifted whatever pavement was once here. Nature is reclaiming this space, but the sign proves humans were here once.

## Hex Shape
Flat-top hexagon tile, isometric perspective. The tree canopy extends slightly above the hex boundary (trees are taller than the ground plane). The base ground fits within the hex footprint.

## Color Palette — Ash & Ember
- Canopy: charred green (#3A4030), with darker and slightly lighter variations
- Bare branches: dark brown-black
- Tree trunks: near-black brown
- Ground cover: very dark olive-brown
- Road sign: rusted orange-brown, faded text
- Moss: muted dark green

## Art Style
Isometric hex tile sprite. The canopy should read as a dense dark mass from above — individual trees suggested but not individually detailed. The ground layer is mostly hidden by canopy. The road sign is a small detail that rewards close inspection. Dark, moody, slightly oppressive.

## World Context
Ash & Ember setting. This forest grew up around and through abandoned infrastructure. Roads, fences, and survey markers have been consumed by root systems. The trees are stressed — partial die-off from whatever event caused the collapse — but still dense enough to provide cover.

## Output
- Transparent PNG
- Isometric hex tile, approximately 120x140 pixels
- Flat-top hexagon orientation
- Tree canopy may extend slightly above the hex top edge for visual depth
- No units — terrain only
