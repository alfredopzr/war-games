# Terrain Tile — Plains (Variant 1)

## Subject
A flat, scorched plain viewed from isometric perspective. Packed, dry earth with fine cracks running across the surface. Scattered small pebbles and gravel. A faint, cracked road stripe (single dashed line) runs diagonally across — a remnant of a highway that was never completed. Minimal vegetation: a few dead grass tufts, dried and flattened.

## Human Presence
Minimal. Only the faint road stripe suggests anyone was ever here. The construction boom reached this area but barely touched it before the collapse.

## Hex Shape
Flat-top hexagon tile, isometric perspective. The tile sits flat on the ground plane with slight depth to the edges (thin visible side faces showing the hex is a slab, not paper-thin).

## Color Palette — Ash & Ember
- Base ground: ashen olive (#6A6A58)
- Crack lines and shadows: darker olive-grey
- Road stripe remnant: faded white/pale grey
- Pebbles: slightly lighter or darker variants of base
- Dead grass: dried yellow-brown, sparse

## Art Style
Isometric hex tile sprite. Detailed enough to read at game zoom but not noisy. Flat areas with subtle texture variation — not perfectly smooth, not overly busy. The ground should feel dry, cracked, and abandoned. Muted, desaturated palette.

## World Context
Ash & Ember setting. This plain was cleared for development that never happened. The road markings were painted but the asphalt was never laid. Dust settles on everything. The air feels still and dry.

## Output
- Transparent PNG
- Isometric hex tile, approximately 120x140 pixels
- Flat-top hexagon orientation
- Suitable for seamless tiling alongside other hex terrain types
- No units, no structures — terrain only
