# Artillery — Engineers (Construction Crane Cannon)

## Subject
A construction crane base converted into a long-range artillery piece. The crane's rotating platform sits on a tracked base. The crane boom has been reinforced and fitted with a long-barreled cannon — the boom arm itself serves as the barrel support structure. Hydraulic stabilizer legs extend outward from the base for recoil absorption. The operator cabin is armored with welded steel plates. Yellow hazard markings everywhere.

## Visual Markers
- Crane base — rotating turret platform on tracks
- Long boom arm — repurposed as cannon barrel mount, extending upward and forward
- Hydraulic stabilizer legs — deployed outward at angles
- Armored operator cabin — small, boxy, welded plates with a slit viewport
- Tall vertical frame — the crane boom dominates the height

## Silhouette
Vertical emphasis. The crane boom / cannon barrel extends high above the tracked base, creating the tallest silhouette of all units. At small sizes, reads as a tall inverted "L" or "T" shape — base on the ground, arm reaching up. The most vertical unit in the Engineers roster.

## Color Palette
- Primary: yellow (#F2C94C) — crane boom, cabin panels, hazard stripes on stabilizer legs
- Secondary: steel grey (#828282) — tracked base, hydraulic cylinders, cannon barrel
- Accent: black (#1A1A1A) — tracks, cabin viewport slit, deep mechanical shadows

## Art Style
Low-poly 3D model, approximately 300-800 triangles. Vertex colored (colors baked into mesh vertices, no texture maps). Flat shaded, no smoothing groups. Distinct geometric parts: tracked base, rotating platform, boom arm, cannon, stabilizer legs, cabin. Inspired by Kenney City Builder asset style but grittier, heavier, and industrial.

## World Context
Ash & Ember setting. This was a tower crane used on megaproject construction sites. Engineers converted the boom into a cannon mount — the crane's ability to rotate and elevate makes it a natural artillery platform. The hydraulic system still works, repurposed for aiming and recoil. Paint peeling, rust streaks, cables dangling.

## Camera & Output
- Camera angle: isometric (30 degrees from horizontal)
- Pre-rendered to 2D sprite
- Output: transparent PNG, 128x128 pixels
- Single isometric view, centered in frame
- Lighting: top-left directional, subtle ambient fill
- No ground plane, no shadow blob — transparent background only
