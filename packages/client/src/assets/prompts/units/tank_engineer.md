# Tank — Engineers (Siege Construction Vehicle)

## Subject
A heavy siege construction vehicle — part bulldozer, part armored assault platform. A massive front bulldozer blade dominates the forward profile. A crane arm folds along the top of the hull, repurposed as a weapon mount. Reinforced steel panels welded over a tracked chassis. Exposed hydraulic pistons along the sides. Hazard-stripe markings on the blade and armor edges.

## Visual Markers
- Bulldozer blade — large, flat, angled forward with welded reinforcement ribs
- Crane arm — folded along the top, with a mounted weapon or grapple at the tip
- Reinforced panels — bolted steel plates with visible rivets
- Hydraulic pistons — exposed along the sides connecting blade to chassis
- Tracked chassis — industrial treads, heavy and wide

## Silhouette
Vertical emphasis. The crane arm and front blade give it height — the crane extends upward, the blade rises above the track line. At small sizes, reads as a tall, front-heavy shape with a vertical protrusion on top. Distinctly taller than the Caravaner War Rig.

## Color Palette
- Primary: yellow (#F2C94C) — bulldozer blade, crane arm, hazard stripes
- Secondary: steel grey (#828282) — hull panels, tracks, hydraulic pistons
- Accent: black (#1A1A1A) — deep shadows, track rubber, exhaust pipes

## Art Style
Low-poly 3D model, approximately 300-800 triangles. Vertex colored (colors baked into mesh vertices, no texture maps). Flat shaded, no smoothing groups. Distinct geometric parts: hull, blade, crane arm, tracks — each clearly readable. Inspired by Kenney City Builder asset style but grittier, weathered, and industrial.

## World Context
Ash & Ember setting. This vehicle was originally a construction machine — bulldozer chassis, crane for lifting girders. Now armored and weaponized by Engineers trying to restart civilization's building projects. The blade clears rubble and enemies alike. Scratched paint, dented panels, dust-caked.

## Camera & Output
- Camera angle: isometric (30 degrees from horizontal)
- Pre-rendered to 2D sprite
- Output: transparent PNG, 128x128 pixels
- Single isometric view, centered in frame
- Lighting: top-left directional, subtle ambient fill
- No ground plane, no shadow blob — transparent background only
